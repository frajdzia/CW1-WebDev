Hello, I am Lidia, BSc Computer Science student at University of Westminster. In this project, I was responsible to create website template and few pages. You can see all of the them by clicking green links below.
Pages edited by me:

  • Home page

    The homepage serves as the gateway to our website, embodying its identity and mission. Central to this page are:

    1. Website Identity: Showcasing our logo, a symbol of our brand and values.
    2. Website Mission: A brief statement that communicates our purpose and what users can expect.
    3. Navigation Bar: A user-friendly menu that provides easy access to the website's sections.
    4. Content Links: Direct links to important content, ensuring information is readily available to visitors.

  • Feedback page

    The feedback page is designed to facilitate smooth communication with our users. Key features include:

    1. Input Fields: For users to easily submit their feedback.
    2. Validation: To ensure the information provided is accurate.
    3. Error Handling: To guide users in correcting submission errors.
    4. Preview Option: Allowing users to review their feedback before final submission.
    5. Edit Functionality: Users can make changes to their feedback before sending.
    6. Confirmation: A message confirming successful feedback submission, enhancing user satisfaction.

  • Content page

    Our content page is meticulously crafted to align with the website's theme, offering:

    1. In-Depth Content: A page of considerable length to support internal linking, enhancing the user experience.
    2. Easy Navigation: Links at the top of the page allow users to jump to different sections, improving accessibility.
    3. Go to Top Button: A prominently placed button that remains visible, enabling users to easily return to the top of the page regardless of their position.

  • Editor page

    Each team member is responsible for designing their own editor page, a space for personal expression and professional showcase, featuring:

    1. Personal Introduction: Displaying the team member's name and role.
    2. Task List: A detailed account of the tasks each member has completed.
    3. Design Freedom: Unlike other pages, the editor page does not need to follow the main website's design. However, my page is focused on main template that I have created for all of the pages, which I am proud of.
    4. Interlinking: Includes a link to the editor page on all pages created by the team member, enhancing connectivity and team visibility.
    5. Links back to the website: Opening in a new tab, it ensures the user's primary interaction with the main site remains uninterrupted and gives a chance to explore individual team contributions.
